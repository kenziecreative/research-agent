Purpose: Users need more than pass/fail from audit-claims. A section can pass (all claims traced) but still have low confidence because it relies on a single low-credibility source with stale data.   <action>
    Read the current audit-claims SKILL.md. Add a new process step 8a (after scorecard generation in step 8, before the audit report in step 9) that computes a per-section confidence tier.

    **Confidence Tier Definitions:**
    - **High**: 4+ independent sources from credible tiers (official docs, analyst reports, peer-reviewed), majority direct evidence, no stale sources dominating
    - **Moderate**: 2-3 independent sources with at least one credible-tier source, mostly direct evidence, stale sources are minority
    - **Low**: 1-2 sources, or sources are primarily low-credibility (vendor marketing, blog posts), or evidence is mostly indirect, or stale sources dominate
    - **Insufficient**: 0-1 sources for a section's claims, or all sources are the same credibility tier with no triangulation

    **Four inputs to assess per section:**
    1. **Source count**: How many independent sources back this section's claims? Map to pattern-recognition-guide levels (Claim=1, Emerging=2-3, Pattern=4+). Echo-level sources (shared origin from cross-ref) count as one.
    2. **Credibility tiers**: What is the highest credibility tier among the section's sources? Mix of tiers is stronger than uniform low-tier.
    3. **Evidence directness**: Are sources directly addressing the claim, or are they adjacent/inferred? Direct = source explicitly states the finding. Indirect = finding is inferred from related data.
    4. **Staleness**: What proportion of the section's sources exceed the research type's staleness threshold (from Plan 01 type templates)? Majority stale = downgrade.

    **Tier computation approach** (Claude's discretion on exact weighting):
    - Start at the source-count level (Claim->Low, Emerging->Moderate, Pattern->High)
    - Upgrade if credibility is strong (all high-tier sources) or evidence is all direct
    - Downgrade if credibility is weak (no high-tier sources), evidence is mostly indirect, or stale sources dominate
    - Cap at Insufficient if fewer than 2 sources with no high-credibility source

    **Add to the scorecard output** (step 8) a new section after severity distribution:
    ```
    Section confidence tiers:
    - [Section name]: [Tier] — [brief rationale: e.g., "3 credible sources, direct evidence, no staleness"]
    - [Section name]: [Tier] — [brief rationale]

    Overall confidence: [Tier of lowest section] (weakest-link determines overall)
    ```

    **Add to the audit report** (step 9): Include the confidence tier table in the audit report written to research/audits/.

    **Modify Pass/Fail Criteria section**: Add a note that confidence tier is informational and does NOT affect pass/fail determination. A section can be High confidence and fail (misrepresented claim) or Low confidence and pass (single source but accurately cited). Add: "Confidence tiers are advisory — they indicate evidence strength, not audit compliance. A Low-confidence section that passes the audit is promoted with its tier visible."

    **Add failure mode row:**
    | Conflating confidence with audit pass/fail — treating low confidence as a failure | Confidence tier measures evidence strength (how well-supported). Audit pass/fail measures evidence accuracy (how truthfully represented). A section with one source, accurately cited, passes the audit with Low confidence. Do not fail it for having thin evidence — flag the tier and let the user decide whether to add sources. |

    **Add guardrail** (next available number): "Do not inflate confidence tiers. If a section relies on a single source, it is Low confidence regardless of how authoritative that source is. Single-source High confidence does not exist — triangulation requires multiple independent sources."
  </action>
